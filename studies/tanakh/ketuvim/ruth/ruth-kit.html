About This Study Kit

This turnkey teaching resource is designed for small groups, Bible studies, or classroom settings. The four-session structure follows Ruth's natural chapter divisions, with each session building on the previous one to reveal the book's theological depth and literary artistry.

Discussion Prompts for Group Study

Q1

Where do you see ◊ó÷∂◊°÷∂◊ì (·∏•esed, covenant love) that costs something?

Guide: Look at Ruth's loyalty (ch.1), Boaz's generosity (ch.2), and the redemption price (ch.4). How does costly love reflect God's character?

Q2

How does the "wing" motif move from prayer (2:12) to fulfillment (3:9)?

Guide: Trace how Boaz's prayer about God's wings becomes his own calling. What does this teach about being answers to our own prayers?

Q3

What does the gate scene teach about public righteousness and community blessing?

Guide: Explore how private faithfulness (chs.1-3) leads to public witness (ch.4). Why does redemption require community involvement?

Q4

How does Naomi's transformation shape your view of God's care for the vulnerable?

Guide: Track her journey from "Mara" to joy. Notice how God works through Ruth and Boaz rather than direct intervention.

Q5

What does Ruth's story reveal about God's inclusion of outsiders?

Guide: Consider Ruth's Moabite identity (Deut 23:3) versus her covenant faithfulness. How does this anticipate the gospel?

üí° Teaching Tips & Best Practices

  • Start with context: Briefly explain the Judges period's darkness to highlight Ruth's faithfulness
  • Use visual aids: Map the Bethlehem-Moab journey; diagram the family tree
  • Emphasize wordplay: Even without Hebrew knowledge, explain key repeated words
  • Make it personal: Connect themes of belonging, loyalty, and providence to modern life
  • Build suspense: Let the narrative tension develop naturally‚Äîdon't rush to applications
  • Honor the outsider: Highlight how Ruth challenges ethnic and social boundaries
  • Connect to Christ: Save explicit messianic connections for the final session's genealogy
