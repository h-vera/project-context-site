Study & Discussion Questions

These questions work for personal study, small groups, or seminary-level classroom discussion. Each is tied to a specific page in the study guide.

  1. Structure: How does the five-part chiastic structure of Deuteronomy (A–B–C–B′–A′) shape the way we read the law collection at the center? Why does Torah sit inside story and exhortation rather than standing alone? (→ Structure page)
  2. Be'er: Moses "makes legible" (be'er) the Torah for a new generation (1:5). What does this tell us about how Scripture should be taught and applied across cultural contexts? (→ Hebrew page)
  3. Shema: The Shema (6:4–5) joins hearing with loving. Why is it significant that "listen" in Hebrew already includes the idea of obedient response? (→ Hebrew / Theology pages)
  4. Heart Arc: Trace the progression from 6:5 → 10:16 → 29:4 → 30:6. How does Deuteronomy move from command to diagnosis to promise? What does this reveal about the Torah's view of human nature? (→ Theology page)
  5. Law as Wisdom: Moses says following the laws will be Israel's "wisdom in the eyes of the peoples" (4:6). How does this change the way we read the law collection — not as compliance but as formative wisdom? (→ Law page)
  6. Blessing & Curse: How are blessing and curse connected to creation/decreation theology rather than arbitrary reward and punishment? How does Deuteronomy 28 echo Genesis 1–3? (→ Theology page)
  7. Land as Danger: Why does Deuteronomy warn that prosperity in the land is the greatest spiritual threat (chs. 6–8)? How does "forgetting" function theologically? (→ Theology / Hebrew pages)
  8. King Under Torah: How does the law of the king (17:14–20) subvert ancient Near Eastern royal ideology? Why must the king write and read Torah daily? (→ Law / Structure pages)
  9. Song of Moses: How does the Song (ch. 32) function as a covenant lawsuit and a prophetic program for all of Israel's future history? (→ Poems page)
  10. Death of Moses: Why does the Torah end with Moses seeing the land but not entering it? What theological point does the narrator make with "no prophet like Moses has arisen … yet"? (→ Poems page)
  11. Every Generation: How does Deuteronomy's "audience blurring" technique make the reader part of the covenant audience? How does the repeated word "today" (הַיּוֹם) compress time? (→ Theology page)
  12. Canon: Choose one NT passage that quotes Deuteronomy (e.g., Jesus' temptation, Romans 10, Galatians 3). How does the NT author interpret and apply the Deuteronomy text? (→ Connections page)
