üî¨ Methodology

This study employs a canonical-literary approach combined with theological synthesis. The methodology integrates multiple disciplines to understand the Psalms as intentionally arranged scripture.

üìñ Canonical Reading

Following Brevard Childs and the BibleProject's approach, this study reads individual psalms within their canonical context. The arrangement of psalms is not random but theologically intentional.

Key Principles:

  • Psalms 1-2 function as a unified two-psalm introduction
  • Psalm 8's position at the center of Psalms 3-14 is interpretive
  • Psalm 148's position at the center of Final Hallel (146-150) is climactic
  • Literary patterns (inclusio, chiasm, positioning) reveal theological meaning

üé® Theological Synthesis

Rather than treating each psalm in isolation, this study synthesizes them into a three-dimensional messianic portrait:

  • Dimension 1: Character (Psalm 1 ‚Äî Torah obedience)
  • Dimension 2: Authority (Psalm 2 ‚Äî Divine kingship)
  • Dimension 3: Method (Psalm 8 ‚Äî Power through weakness)
  • Culmination: Victory (Psalm 148 ‚Äî Promise fulfilled)

This integration prevents incomplete or distorted messianic expectations by showing how all three dimensions work together.
