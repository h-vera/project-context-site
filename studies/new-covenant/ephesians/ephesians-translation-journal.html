This journal documents translation and structural decisions made in creating the Literal–Literary Structured Edition of Ephesians. It records Greek text analysis, alternative renderings, lexical rationale, structural formatting, and theological coherence considerations based on Tim Mackie's translation for The BibleProject.

Methodological Note: This journal reconstructs translation decisions by comparing the final English rendering against NA28/UBS5 Greek text. Since this is a retrospective analysis of Tim Mackie's work (not documentation by the original translator), lexical alternatives and rationales are inferred from:

  • The stated translation philosophy: "literal where possible, literary where necessary"
  • Observable patterns across the complete translation
  • The BibleProject's known translation principles
  • Hebraic thought patterns highlighted in the formatting
  • Standard lexical resources (BDAG, LSJ, Louw-Nida)

Where uncertainty exists, it is acknowledged. This journal serves educational purposes, making translation methodology visible for students and teachers.

Translation Philosophy

This translation employs a literal–literary style: close to Greek wording while surfacing Hebraic cadence (triads, parallelism, creed/doxology). This is not a paraphrase or commentary but a reconstruction of how the text would have been heard by its earliest audience.

Base Text: NA28/UBS5 Greek (critical text)

Priority Hierarchy: Discourse function → Lexical core → Hebraic echoes → Rhythm & register → Consistency → Clarity

Key Features

Chapter Highlights

  • Chapter 1: Extended blessing formula (berakah), prayer for wisdom, resurrection power, cosmic Christ
  • Chapter 2: From death to life, Jew-Gentile reconciliation, church as God's temple
  • Chapter 3: Mystery revealed (Gentiles as co-heirs), cosmic wisdom, climactic doxology (structural hinge)
  • Chapter 4: Seven unities, gifts for maturity, old/new humanity contrast
  • Chapter 5: Walk in love and light, Spirit-filled living, household codes
  • Chapter 6: Armor of God, spiritual warfare, final greetings

Structural Anchors

Major liturgical/rhetorical markers that shape the letter's architecture:

  • 1:3-14: Opening blessing (berakah) — one extended Greek sentence with three refrains
  • 3:20-21: Doxology — structural hinge between theology (chs. 1-3) and ethics (chs. 4-6)
  • Recurring phrase: "ἐν τοῖς ἐπουρανίοις" (in the heavenlies) — 5x throughout letter

Methodology & Sources

Greek Text: NA28 / UBS5 (critical text base)

Lexica: BDAG (primary), LSJ (classical background), Louw–Nida (semantic domains)

Discourse Analysis: Runge & Westbury (discourse grammar), Porter (verbal aspect)

Visual Conventions:

  • Indentation shows logical relationships and subordinate clauses
  • Color-coding marks distinct rhetorical units (blessing, mystery, reconciliation, cosmic themes)
  • Block spacing separates distinct thought units
  • Visual borders highlight structural anchors (doxologies, blessing formulas)
