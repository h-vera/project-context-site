Aramaic Chiasm (Daniel 2-7)

Chapters 2-7 form a symmetrical pattern with the Son of Man vision at the center:

A Ch 2: Dream of statue ‚Äî four kingdoms crushed by God's kingdom (stone ‚Üí mountain)
B Ch 3: Faithfulness test ‚Äî three friends in fiery furnace, delivered
C Ch 4: Nebuchadnezzar's pride ‚Äî becomes beast, then restored
C‚Ä≤ Ch 5: Belshazzar's pride ‚Äî writing on wall, kingdom falls
B‚Ä≤ Ch 6: Faithfulness test ‚Äî Daniel in lions' den, delivered
A‚Ä≤ Ch 7: Dream of beasts ‚Äî four kingdoms judged, Son of Man receives kingdom
CENTER: Chapters 4-5 ‚Äî Human kings become beasts when they reject God
FRAME: Chapters 2 & 7 ‚Äî God's kingdom ultimately triumphs over beastly empires
Design Insight: The chiasm places the "beast/human" contrast at the center ‚Äî Nebuchadnezzar literally becomes a beast, then is restored when he acknowledges God. This prepares for Daniel 7 where arrogant empires are portrayed as beasts, while the faithful "Son of Man" (true human) receives the kingdom.

üìñ Narrative Summary

Daniel's story unfolds in two movements:

Part 1: Court Tales (Ch 1-6)

  • Ch 1: Exile to Babylon, faithfulness test (food laws), elevation
  • Ch 2: Nebuchadnezzar's dream ‚Äî statue of kingdoms, stone becomes mountain
  • Ch 3: Friends refuse to worship idol, survive furnace
  • Ch 4: Nebuchadnezzar becomes beast, then restored
  • Ch 5: Belshazzar's feast, writing on wall, kingdom falls
  • Ch 6: Daniel in lions' den, delivered, king praises God

Part 2: Prophetic Visions (Ch 7-12)

  • Ch 7: Four beasts, Ancient of Days, Son of Man receives kingdom
  • Ch 8: Ram and goat vision ‚Äî Medo-Persia and Greece
  • Ch 9: Daniel's prayer, seventy sevens revealed
  • Ch 10-12: Final vision ‚Äî kings of north/south, "time of distress," resurrection
Narrative Pattern: Daniel experiences elevation ‚Üí opposition ‚Üí testing ‚Üí divine rescue ‚Üí revelation ‚Üí renewed hope ‚Äî mirroring Israel's exilic journey and pointing toward the ultimate deliverance through the Son of Man.
