Summary: Abigail emerges in the David narratives as a model of wisdom and diplomacy. Faced with Nabal's insult to David, she courageously intervenes, averting bloodshed by offering provisions and a humble plea. Her quick action preserves her household, prevents David from sinning, and secures her place as one of David's wives after Nabal's death.

Theological Significance: Abigail embodies the biblical theme that God opposes folly but exalts wisdom. Her role foreshadows Christ's work as mediatorâ€”standing in the breach to turn aside wrath and bring peace.
