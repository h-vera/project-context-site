💎 Key Theological Insights

Central themes woven throughout the 8-page study

🔄

Divine Reversal

God exalts the humble and opposes the proud. David's journey from overlooked shepherd to anointed king embodies Hannah's prophetic song and becomes the pattern for messianic hope.

📖 1 Sam 2:1-10; 16:7
🏛️

Priestly-Royal Fusion

David's ark procession and Zion worship establish priestly kingship, anticipating Psalm 110's "priest forever" and Jesus as the ultimate mediator who unites throne and altar.

📖 2 Sam 6-7; Ps 110
💔

Covenant Amid Failure

David's moral collapse doesn't erase God's covenant promise. Forgiveness without consequence-erasure reveals grace that sustains hope for a future, faithful Son of David.

📖 2 Sam 11-12; Ps 51
🎵

Poetry as Theology

David's psalms transform personal experience into universal worship, creating a liturgical framework that shapes Israel's faith and anticipates Christ's victory over death.

📖 2 Sam 22; Ps 2, 18, 110
✨

Messianic Trajectory

The 2 Samuel 7 covenant becomes Scripture's load-bearing beam— an eternal dynasty, a temple-building son, and a kingdom that culminates in Jesus, the true Son of David.

📖 2 Sam 7; Luke 1:32-33; Acts 2
🗻

Cosmic Mountain Vision

From Goliath's valley to Zion's summit to the temple threshing floor, David's geography traces Eden-restoration—chaos subdued, presence established, creation renewed on the holy mountain.

📖 1 Sam 17; 2 Sam 24; Ps 24
