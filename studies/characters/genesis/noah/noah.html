üìñ What Noah's Story Teaches

Noah's narrative answers a crucial question: What does God do when creation fails? The answer is neither simple destruction nor naive optimism. Instead, God responds with judgment that preserves, a reset that acknowledges ongoing human failure, and a covenant that commits to creation's future despite knowing the human heart remains unchanged.

The flood solves the problem of immediate violence by removing corrupt society. But it does not solve the deeper problem‚Äîthe human heart. That tension sets the stage for God's covenant strategy and the eventual call of Abraham.

Noah explains how humanity survives. Abraham will explain how humanity will be blessed.
