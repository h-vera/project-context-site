Summary: An unnamed Israelite girl, taken captive in a Syrian raid, served in the household of Naaman, commander of Syria's army. Despite her circumstances, she demonstrated remarkable faith and compassion by directing her leprous master to the prophet Elisha for healing, catalyzing one of Scripture's most significant gentile conversion narratives.

Theological Significance: This young captive embodies God's sovereignty in using the powerless to accomplish his purposes, demonstrating that faith transcends circumstances and that God's healing extends beyond Israel to all nations.
