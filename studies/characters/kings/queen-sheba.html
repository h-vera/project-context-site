Summary: The Queen of Sheba, drawn by reports of Solomon's wisdom and fame concerning the name of Yahweh, journeys to Jerusalem with great wealth to test him with riddles. Witnessing his wisdom, justice, and the abundance of his kingdom, she is overwhelmed and blesses Yahweh for establishing Solomon as king to execute justice and righteousness.

Theological Significance: She embodies the nations recognizing Yahweh's wisdom and blessing, fulfilling the Abrahamic promise that all nations would be blessed through Abraham's seed (Gen 12:3). Her doxology anticipates prophetic visions of the nations streaming to Zion to worship Israel's God.

Narrative Journey

Journey with Riddles (1 Kings 10:1-3): Having heard of Solomon's fame "concerning the name of the LORD," she comes to test him with hard questions. Solomon answers all her riddles; nothing is hidden from his understanding, demonstrating that divine wisdom surpasses human cleverness.
Tour of Abundance (1 Kings 10:4-8): She observes Solomon's wisdom in action: his house, his table's provisions, his officials' seating arrangement, his servants' attire, his cupbearers, and his burnt offerings at the temple. Her breath is literally "taken away" as she realizes the reports fell short of reality.
Doxology to Yahweh (1 Kings 10:9): In the climactic moment, she blesses Yahweh directly: "Blessed be the LORD your God, who has delighted in you and set you on the throne of Israel! Because the LORD loved Israel forever, he has made you king, that you may execute justice and righteousness."
Royal Exchange (1 Kings 10:10, 13): She gives Solomon 120 talents of gold, precious spices, and stones. Solomon reciprocates with gifts befitting her royal status plus "all that she desired." This mutual exchange signals peaceful diplomatic relations and shared prosperity.
Pattern Recognition: The narrative presents her as a foil to Solomon's later foreign wives (1 Kings 11). While she seeks wisdom and blesses Yahweh, they will lead Solomon into idolatry. She represents the ideal foreign response to Israel's covenant blessings.

Ancient Near Eastern Context & Biblical Distinctives

üìú ANE Parallels

  • Royal Visits: Diplomatic journeys with tribute were common for establishing trade relations and political alliances.
  • Riddle Contests: Testing wisdom through riddles was a recognized form of royal diplomatic engagement.
  • Gift Exchange: Reciprocal giving established and maintained peaceful international relations.

‚ö° Biblical Distinctives

  • Theological Recognition: She explicitly blesses Israel's God, not just Israel's king.
  • Moral Wisdom: Focus on justice and righteousness, not just clever answers.
  • Covenant Frame: Her blessing acknowledges Yahweh's eternal love for Israel.
