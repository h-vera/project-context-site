Team Charter

The constitution of our formation team: purpose, commitments, guardrails, and how men and women practice faithfully together in Acts-shaped community.

This charter grounds our work together. It clarifies what we're training for, how we'll operate, and the cultural commitments that keep us safe, humble, and Scripture-centered.

üéØ Purpose

This formation team exists to discern, develop, and practice Spirit-gifted proclamation together‚Äîincluding teaching, testimony, exhortation, and prophetic encouragement‚Äîfor the building up of the church and its witness in the world.

Shaped by the Book of Acts, we train as a "speaking faithfully team," recognizing that proclamation in the early church was often shared, sequential, and communal, not isolated or individual.

üïäÔ∏è Acts-Shaped Vision

In Acts, God regularly sends people together to speak:

  • Barnabas and Paul enter cities as a team, taking turns speaking and strengthening believers
  • Peter speaks with the Eleven standing beside him
  • Prophetic voices speak within community and are discerned together
  • Teaching, testimony, and exhortation occur in synagogues, homes, public spaces, and journeys
We therefore reject a lone-performer model of preaching. Instead, we practice proclamation as a shared ministry, where individuals may speak but never stand alone.

üìñ Theological Commitments

Jesus-centered

All proclamation points to the risen and reigning Jesus and his kingdom.

Scripture-anchored

All speech is rooted in careful attention to the biblical text.

Spirit-dependent

We expect the Spirit to guide preparation, speaking, and discernment.

Church-building

Speech aims to strengthen, encourage, and console.

Orderly & accountable

All contributions are open to communal testing and refinement.

Humble & Christ-exalting

We redirect praise to God, never to ourselves.

üë• Men and Women in the Formation Team

Following the witness of Acts, this team affirms that men and women are gifted by the Spirit to speak God's word in a variety of faithful contexts.

What We Affirm

  • Philip's daughters prophesied (Acts 21:9)
  • Joel 2 promised the Spirit on sons and daughters
  • Prophecy is a Spirit gift, not a gendered privilege
  • Gifting does not automatically equal platform or authority

How We Practice

  • Make space for women and men to practice proclamation
  • Identify appropriate and faithful settings
  • Honor conscience while refusing to silence Spirit-given gifts
  • Focus on formation, not resolving church polity debates

Without forcing agreement on contested questions of church order, we provide a formation space where all participants can grow in their Spirit-given gifts.

ü§ù Shared Formation & Mutual Discernment

This team understands proclamation as a communal work. While individuals may be tasked to speak publicly, preparation, shaping, and review are shared.

Core principle: Feedback is offered before and after speaking, always for formation‚Äînot evaluation.

Before Speaking

Team helps refine the Big Idea, clarify movements, and identify where Jesus is clearest.

During Speaking

Team listens for clarity, Jesus-centeredness, and genuine edification.

After Speaking

Team offers affirmation, one clarity theme, and one growth invitation.

These commitments protect the team from common pitfalls and keep our culture safe, humble, and formative:

  • No one preaches alone ‚Äì Preparation is communal; no solo building
  • No shaming, ranking, or comparison ‚Äì We celebrate each person's unique growth
  • No use of "God told me" to shut down discernment ‚Äì All speech is testable
  • Feedback begins with affirmation ‚Äì We name what's working before addressing growth areas
  • Growth is expected; perfection is not ‚Äì We're in formation, not performance
  • Humility, teachability, and love are marks of maturity ‚Äì Character matters more than eloquence
