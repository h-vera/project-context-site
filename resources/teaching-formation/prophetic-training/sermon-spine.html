Sermon Spine

A shared, repeatable framework based on Acts sermon patterns. Every message moves through text ‚Üí context ‚Üí Jesus ‚Üí response.

üß© The Master Framework

Acts sermons follow a consistent pattern, regardless of speaker or audience. This spine gives you the same reliable structure:

1. Anchor in Text

Stay within one passage

  • Choose a single passage or narrative unit
  • Preferably from the same biblical book
  • Read it aloud to the congregation

2. Brief Context

Quick but precise

  • Where are we in the story?
  • Who is speaking‚Äîand to whom?
  • What tension or question is being addressed?

3. Big Idea

One clear sentence

  • State the main point in one sentence
  • Make it clear enough for anyone to remember
  • Let it arise from the text, not your agenda

4. Movements (2‚Äì4)

Walk through the text

  • Break the passage into logical sections
  • Show how each movement advances the Big Idea
  • Keep explanations clear and connected

5. Jesus at the Center

Non-negotiable

  • Show where Jesus is clearest in this text
  • Connect to death, resurrection, or exaltation
  • Avoid moralism‚Äîpoint to the gospel

6. Response Invitation

Concrete & gracious

  • Give one clear, doable response
  • Make it specific, not vague
  • Invite with grace, not guilt

7. Closing Prayer

Ask the Spirit to work

  • Pray for hearts to receive what God has said
  • Ask for the Spirit's power to respond faithfully
  • Keep it simple and heartfelt

üìö Acts Sermon Types

Acts shows us different sermon forms, all using the same spine:

Expository / Text-Driven

Example: Peter (Acts 2), Paul (Acts 13)

Explain Scripture passage by passage, showing how it points to Jesus.

Narrative-Historical

Example: Stephen (Acts 7)

Retell Israel's story as one theological argument leading to Jesus.

Evangelistic / Apologetic

Example: Paul in synagogues & marketplaces

Reason from Scripture to show Jesus is the promised Messiah.

Testimonial / Witness

Example: Paul's conversion accounts

Share personal encounter with Jesus as proclamation (Before ‚Üí Encounter ‚Üí Now).

Prophetic Encouragement

Example: Philip's daughters (Acts 21:9)

Build up the church with Spirit-prompted words of strength and hope.

All these types use the same spine. The difference is in emphasis and style, not structure.

Before Practice

  • Share Big Idea with team
  • Outline 2‚Äì4 movements
  • Identify where Jesus is clearest
  • Ask team for forming feedback

During Practice
