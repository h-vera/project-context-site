Wisdom Blesses the One at the Door

Tonight is not about having it figured out. It is about showing up ready to listen, seek, and be formed.

"Your academics aren't just about grades — they're part of how you discover who God made you to be and how you'll help the world flourish."

"Blessed is the one who listens to me, watching daily at my doors, waiting at my doorway. For those who find me find life and receive favor from the Lord." Proverbs 8:34–35
Leader Note Read it twice — once you, once someone else. The frame is simple: wisdom blesses the seeker, not the expert. Return to this verse at the close.
Opening
🌿 Ice Breaker

Before You Had It Figured Out

5–7 minutes · pair share, then open

We all carry moments where we were handed something we were not ready for. That is actually the starting point for everything we are going to talk about tonight.

  • 1
    Think of a time — school, work, relationships — when you genuinely did not know what to do next. What did you do?
  • 2
    Who is someone you respect who was openly still figuring things out — and did that make you trust them more or less?
Block 1 · Formation
💬 Discussion

Academics Is Not the Whole Picture

10–12 minutes

College opens up swim lanes — majors, minors, electives. But it does not tell you which lane is yours. That is a different kind of learning entirely.

Genesis 1–2 gives us the frame: God created the world good — but not finished. He then handed humans the work of developing it. Keller's line lands here: "God didn't create a finished world — He created a world with potential, and He invites us into it." Your studies are part of that invitation.

  • 1
    Where do you feel most alive in your work or studies? Not what you're best at — where do you feel like yourself?
  • 2
    When does school pull you away from God the most? Pressure, comparison, performance anxiety — name it honestly.
Leader Note The frame: academics is a subset of education. You're learning subjects, but also learning yourself. The Genesis move connects study to something bigger than a transcript — it's participation in God's ongoing work of development. Let that land before moving on.
Block 2 · Self-Discovery
💬 Discussion

What Do You Love Enough to Lose Track of Time?

10–12 minutes

There is a framework called the Flower Diagram in What Color Is Your Parachute? — it maps what energizes you, the people you want to work with, and where your gifts meet real need. College gives you the lanes; self-knowledge tells you which ones to swim in.

  • 1
    What do you love doing so much that time disappears?
  • 2
    Does your current path connect to that at all — or are you mostly following what looks good on paper? Keller: vocation sits where your gifts, the world's need, and what you find meaningful intersect. Which of those three is haziest right now?
"Before I formed you in the womb I knew you, before you were born I set you apart." Jeremiah 1:5 — Your identity precedes your résumé.
Block 3 · Humility
💬 Discussion

"I Don't Know" Can Be a Strength

12–15 minutes

Everyone who has ever worked hits the wall of not knowing — again and again throughout a career. The question is what you do with it.

Ecclesiastes names it honestly: work can feel meaningless, repetitive, frustrating. Keller says that's not a sign you chose the wrong path — it's the reality of a fallen world. Even meaningful work won't fully satisfy. So: "If you expect school or your future job to fully fulfill you — it will eventually disappoint you."

Culture A
"I don't know" is not safe — people fake it or go quiet.
Culture B
"I don't know — let's figure it out together." Trust grows here.
The Posture
"I don't know, but I'll find out." That makes you valuable in any culture.
  • 1
    When you don't know something, what do you usually do?
  • 2
    What is the difference between "I don't know" and "I don't know, but I'll find out"? Where do you feel the most pressure to perform certainty right now?
"The beginning of wisdom is this: Get wisdom, and whatever you get, get insight." Proverbs 4:7 — Wisdom starts with acknowledging you don't yet have it.
Leader Note College trains the wrong reflex — right answers get rewarded, uncertainty gets penalized. The workplace and the gospel both invert this. Share a brief story from your own career if it opens the room.
Block 4 · Identity
💬 Discussion

The Front and the Back of the Jersey

10–12 minutes

Coach Krzyzewski told his Duke players: you represent what is on the front of the jersey — the institution, the team — and what is on the back — your name, the people who got you there. Identity gets distorted when one side swallows the other.

Front School · Major · Job Institution · Team · Brand
Back Your Name Family · People · Calling
  • 1
    What is on the front of your jersey right now — and what is on the back?
  • 2
    What tends to become your identity — grades, success, or something else? Keller: grades can become identity, career can become worth, money/status can become the goal. Cultural lie — "do what makes the most money or gets the most attention." Truth — "do what honors God and serves others with how He made you."
"Where your treasure is, there your heart will be also." Matthew 6:21 — What you orbit reveals what you worship.
Leader Note Over-identifying with the front of the jersey is how work becomes an idol. The gospel means your ultimate identity is held by Someone else — which frees you to take both sides seriously without being crushed by either.

🤝 Reference Box

Mentorship: Two Lanes Worth Pursuing

Surface this in the closing or after the jersey discussion. "Who in your life is investing in you — not just your career, but your character?" and "Who do you want to become — and who is helping you get there?"

Two Lanes
SpiritualSomeone who helps you not wrap your identity around the wrong things. A person who sees your life from above the noise.
VocationalAn informational interview — 20 minutes of someone's story. Not a job ask. Most people want to be asked. One email this week.
"Walk with the wise and become wise, for a companion of fools suffers harm." Proverbs 13:20 — Proximity shapes you. Choose it intentionally.
Closing
✦ Sending Question

Keep Showing Up at the Door

Wisdom blesses the one who keeps showing up — curious, humble, and ready to be formed. Not the one who arrived.

"Blessed is the one who listens to me, watching daily at my doors, waiting at my doorway." Proverbs 8:34
What is one thing you want to do differently this week — in how you approach your studies, your work, or your sense of who you are?

"You're not just preparing for a career — you're preparing to partner with God in restoring His world."

Leader Note Go around the room if possible. Push for something specific — a name to reach out to, a habit to change, a conversation to have. Close in prayer, naming what people shared.
