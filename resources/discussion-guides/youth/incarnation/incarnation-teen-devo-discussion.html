üî• God Keeps Showing Up

Even before Jesus was born in Bethlehem, God found ways to show up in forms people could see.

Read: Joshua 5:13-15 "When Joshua was near Jericho, he looked up and saw a man standing in front of him with a drawn sword. Joshua asked, 'Are you for us or for our enemies?' 'Neither,' he replied, 'but as commander of the army of the LORD I have now come.' Then Joshua fell facedown in reverence‚Ä¶"
  • Joshua asks whose side this person is on. The answer? "Neither‚ÄîI'm the Commander of the LORD's army." What does that say about God's priorities?
  • Joshua worships this figure and isn't stopped‚Äîmany scholars think this is God showing up in human form before the Incarnation. Why would God do that?

But Notice What Happens with Angels and Humans...

Throughout Scripture, when people try to worship angels or other humans, they're stopped immediately:

Acts 10:25-26 ‚Äì When Cornelius tries to worship Peter, Peter says: "Stand up; I am only a man myself."
Acts 14:11-15 ‚Äì When people try to worship Paul and Barnabas as gods, they tear their clothes and shout: "We too are only human, like you!"
Revelation 19:10 & 22:8-9 ‚Äì When John tries to worship an angel, the angel says: "Don't do that! I am a fellow servant... Worship God!"

Here's the big deal: Angels refuse worship. Apostles refuse worship. But in Joshua 5, this figure accepts worship and tells Joshua to take off his sandals because the ground is holy. God has always wanted to be with His people. The Incarnation is when He goes all the way and becomes one of us.

üí¨ 2. God Spoke in Many Ways... But Now

Before Jesus, God communicated through dreams, visions, prophets, angels, and miraculous encounters. But something radical changed.

Read: Hebrews 1:1-2 "In the past God spoke to our ancestors through the prophets at many times and in various ways, but in these last days he has spoken to us by his Son, whom he appointed heir of all things, and through whom also he made the universe."

How Did God Speak Before?

üî• Burning Bush God spoke to Moses from a bush that burned but wasn't consumed (Exodus 3)
ü§º Wrestling God wrestled with Jacob all night until daybreak (Genesis 32)
üí≠ Dreams God gave Abraham visions and dreams about the future (Genesis 15)
‚ú® Visions Prophets like Isaiah and Ezekiel saw visions of God's throne room
üìú Prophets God spoke through Moses, Samuel, Elijah, Jeremiah, and many others
üëº Angels Heavenly messengers delivered God's words to His people
The Big Shift: All these ways were good‚Äîbut they were partial, temporary, and indirect. But NOW God has spoken through His Son. Not just words through a prophet. Not just a vision in a dream. Not just an angel with a message. The Word Himself became flesh.
  • Why do you think God used so many different ways to communicate before Jesus came?
  • What's the difference between getting a message from God versus God Himself showing up as a human?
  • If Jesus is God's final and clearest message to us, what does that say about how important it is to actually know Him?

God wanted a relationship with humanity, not just to give us instructions. He partnered with people throughout history, but they kept failing. So finally, God Himself stepped into the story in the most direct way possible.

üí≠ 5. Group Reflection: What Would God Be Like?

Coming back together as a whole group for one big question.

Discussion Question: What would God be like if He DIDN'T become human?
  • Would God feel distant or close?
  • Would we know how to actually live as His followers?
  • Would we just have rules, or would we have a living example?
  • Would we believe He really understands what we go through?
  • Would we be equipped to share His love with others?
The Point: Because God became human, you can trust Him, relate to Him, and follow Him. You're not guessing who God is‚ÄîHe showed you. Jesus is God in a form you can understand. And He didn't just come to be with us‚ÄîHe came to send us out as His witnesses to bless the world.

üö∂ 6. One Simple Next Step

Silent Reflection: If Jesus stood beside you tonight, what would He say about the way you're living?

Before you scroll away tonight, take 60 seconds and talk to Jesus like a real person:

  • Tell Him one thing that confuses you about God becoming human.
  • Tell Him one place you want Him to be more real in your life this week.
  • Ask Him how He wants to use you to bless someone else this week.

You don't have to sound "churchy." Just be honest. If God spoke His clearest message through His Son, you're allowed to talk to Him like a real person.

Remember: The incarnation means God didn't stay distant. He became human so you could know Him, trust Him, walk with Him, and join His mission to bless the world. God came as one of us so we could become like Him and share His love with others.
