üéØ The Vision (What We're Building Together)

Our teen ministry exists to form young disciples of Jesus through meaningful relationships, embodied faith, and shared experiences‚Äîwithin the real cultural world they live in today.

This gives us permission to keep learning, seek outside input, experiment, listen first, and trust teens with agency.

üí¨ What Teens Are Already Telling Us

Building on conversations from December‚Äîthis is discipleship hunger, not rebellion.

They want:

  • Hands-on faith: Reaching out to people, inviting them to church, living out discipleship in practical ways (the December devo brainstorming was a highlight‚Äîteens bonding, getting excited, and generating ideas together)
  • Worship that feels real: Music, instruments, emotional engagement with God

  • Leadership opportunities: We have mature teen disciples ready to lead

The foundation: When teens led games and started brainstorming together in December, something clicked‚Äîthey were engaged, bonding, and excited about living out their faith. That energy is what we're building on.

ü§ù Community & Partnership

Teen ministry works best when parents, leaders, and teens collaborate

Local Partnerships

  • Parent nights (monthly): Equipping parents with resources, language, and confidence
  • Cross-generational connections: Service projects that involve families, not just teens
  • Shared meals & experiences: Building the faith ecosystem beyond Sunday mornings

What Adults Must Be Doing

If leaders aren't growing, the ministry stagnates, no matter how faithful they are.

Every adult involved with teens should be:

  • Learning from outside voices (see Resources section below)
  • Listening to teens and parents
  • Sharing ideas, not just running programs
  • Modeling faith, not managing behavior
Question for reflection: What are we as adults doing to understand this generation? We can't disciple teens well using instincts formed 30 years ago.

üë™ Parent Input

We're asking parents to share their perspective and ask their teens

Questions for parents

  • Belonging: Do your teens feel known and connected? If not, what would help?
  • Discipleship: Where do teens need the most support (prayer, purity, friends, identity, stress, etc.)?
  • Worship: What would meaningful worship look like for them (music, participation, teen leadership)?
  • Hands-on faith: What kinds of service / faith-in-action opportunities would engage them?
  • Time together: How often should we aim for non-Sunday time together (monthly? twice a month?)
  • Communication: What's the best way to communicate with parents and teens (text threads, GroupMe, email)?

Ask parents to ask their teens

First Sundays (Teen Class): Please encourage your teen to bring a musical instrument of their choice (if they want). We'd love to incorporate singing with instruments and create space for worship that helps teens emotionally engage with God.

We want teen voice, not adult assumptions.

  • "When do you feel most connected at church?"
  • "What makes teen ministry feel real and not forced?"
  • "What's one thing you wish adults understood about your world?"
  • "If you could change one thing from now to June, what would it be?"
