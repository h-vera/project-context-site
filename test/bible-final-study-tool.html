Essay Outlines — Memorize These Structures

Essay #1 — The Deuterocanon (Q32 — 14% — highest value)

1. Topic + name all 7: Tobit, Judith, 1 Maccabees, 2 Maccabees, Wisdom, Sirach, Baruch
2. Reason 1: Catholics/Orthodox based OT on Greek Septuagint; Protestants used shorter Hebrew canon (~90 AD)
3. Reason 2: Written late (200–50 BC), some in Greek → Luther and reformers questioned their authority
4. Bonus: Catholics confirmed them at Council of Trent, 1546
5. Close: Catholic OT = 46 books; Protestant OT = 39 books

Essay #2 — Three Kinds of Old Testament Law (Q11)

1. Topic + name all three: Moral Law, Ceremonial Law, Civil Law
2. Moral Law: Ten Commandments + timeless right/wrong → still applies; God's unchanging character; reaffirmed by Jesus
3. Ceremonial Law: sacrifices, feasts, priestly duties → no longer; Jesus fulfilled it as the final sacrifice
4. Civil Law: governed Israel as a nation-state → no longer; the Church is not a government
5. Closing sentence

Essay #3 — Jesus and the Passover Lamb (Q4)

1. “Jesus is similar to the Passover Lamb in several important ways.”
2. Original Passover: unblemished lamb + blood on doorposts + Angel of Death passes over + freedom from Egypt
3. Jesus: “Lamb of God” — sinless, sacrificed, blood saves from sin (not just physical death)
4. Freedom: lamb → freedom from Egypt; Jesus → freedom from slavery to sin
5. Close: Not coincidental Jesus was crucified during Passover week

Essay Bonus — Role of a Biblical Prophet (mentioned in answer key)

1. Primary role: speak God's word, call people back to faithfulness — NOT mainly fortune-tellers
2. Message 1: Warning/challenge — if people kept sinning, there would be consequences (judgment, exile)
3. Message 2: Hope/restoration — even after judgment, God would not abandon them
4. Example: Jeremiah was thrown in a cistern; Isaiah served kings who ignored him — rarely popular
5. Close: faithfulness despite rejection is central to their witness

Essay #5 — Red Sea, Noah's Ark & Baptism (Q5)

1. Red Sea: God parted the sea; Israel crossed on dry ground; Egyptian army drowned when waters returned
2. Noah connection: In Noah's flood, water destroyed the wicked while saving the faithful — same pattern as the Red Sea
3. Baptism connection: water symbolizes passing from death (old life/sin) to new life in Christ — same death-and-resurrection pattern
4. All three: water = judgment on evil + salvation and new life for the faithful
5. Close: Church Fathers called the Red Sea a “type” of Baptism

Essay #6 — Babylonian Captivity Timeline (Q2 Unit 7)

1. 722 BC: Assyria conquered the northern Kingdom of Israel; scattered the 10 tribes
2. 586 BC: Nebuchadnezzar of Babylon conquered Judah, destroyed Solomon's Temple, exiled the people — called the Babylonian Captivity
3. ~538 BC: Cyrus the Great of Persia conquered Babylon; issued a decree allowing the exiles to return and rebuild
4. Rebuilding books: Ezra (spiritual rebuilding/restoring the Law) and Nehemiah (physical rebuilding of the walls)
5. Key names: Nebuchadnezzar (destroyer), Cyrus the Great (restorer)

Essay #7 — Job and Suffering (Q19)

1. Job is a biblical novella about innocent suffering — Job did nothing wrong but lost everything
2. Lesson 1: Suffering is NOT always punishment for sin — Job's friends were wrong to assume it was
3. Lesson 2: God is present in suffering even when we do not understand it
4. God's answer from the whirlwind: “Where were you when I laid the foundations of the earth?” — his ways exceed our understanding
5. Biblical arc: God's ultimate response to suffering is Jesus — God himself enters human suffering in the Incarnation

Essay #8 — Division of the Kingdom (Q8 Unit 6)

1. After Solomon's death, his son Rehoboam became king
2. The 10 northern tribes asked Rehoboam to lighten their heavy labor burden; he threatened to make it heavier
3. Split: 10 northern tribes formed Kingdom of Israel under Jeroboam; 2 southern tribes remained as Kingdom of Judah under Rehoboam
4. Long-term: Israel (north) fell to Assyria in 722 BC; Judah (south) fell to Babylon in 586 BC
5. Root cause: Rehoboam's refusal to lighten the people's burdens — pride and poor leadership
